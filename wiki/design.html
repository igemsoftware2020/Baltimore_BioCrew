Materials and Methods


  • Characterization of Cyanobacteria Growth in Iron v

    To test the significance of iron amounts to the phytoplankton population, we used Synechococcus CB0101 (initial OD 0.05) and put it into 5 conical tubes with SN media with varying amounts of iron concentrations: No iron, 0.01X, 0.1X, 1X (0.023 mM), and 10X normal iron concentration. The growth of these tubes were measured by optical density. CB0101 was grown under light intensity measuring 1000-1700 Lux and in an incubator maintaining the temperature of 20-35℃.

  • Improving Iron Consumption Efficiency in Cyanobacteria v
    1. We want to understand the effect of iron concentrations on cyanobacteria growth. We will characterize cyanobacteria growth by using modeling and experimental measurements.
    2. We want to modify Synechocystis sp. strain CB0101 to have the ability to grow in conditions with lower iron. This will be accomplished by adding iron transport, uptake, and binding genes from other cyanobacteria strains and species that evolved in iron poor environments. We will also modify the strength of an iron-sensitive promoter.
    3. We want to use safe and ethical practices in modifying organisms. We will prevent phytoplankton blooms that are bad for the environment by engineering a safety mechanism so that our cyanobacteria die in environments with very high iron.
